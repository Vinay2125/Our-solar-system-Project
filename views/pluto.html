Pluto

Pluto was once considered the ninth planet in our solar system, but it's now classified as a dwarf planet.

Key Characteristics

  • Orbital Period: Approximately 248 Earth years
  • Diameter: About 2,377 kilometers (1,477 miles)
  • Surface Features: Pluto has a diverse surface with icy mountains and a large heart-shaped region called Tombaugh Regio.

Discovery and Exploration

Pluto was discovered in 1930 by astronomer Clyde Tombaugh. In 2015, NASA's New Horizons spacecraft provided detailed images and data about Pluto during its flyby.

Interesting Facts

  • Pluto has a thin atmosphere primarily composed of nitrogen.
  • Its moon Charon is unusually large in comparison to Pluto and forms a binary system with it.
