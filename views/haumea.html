Haumea: Dwarf Planet

Discovery

Haumea was discovered on December 28, 2004, by a team of astronomers led by Jos√© Luis Ortiz Moreno at the Sierra Nevada Observatory in Spain. Its discovery was officially announced in July 2005.

Location

Haumea is located in the Kuiper Belt, a region of the solar system beyond the orbit of Neptune that is home to many small icy objects.

Characteristics

  • Shape: Haumea has an elongated, football-like shape, making it one of the most oblate objects in the solar system.
  • Diameter: Its dimensions are approximately 2,320 kilometers (1,440 miles) in length, 1,518 kilometers (943 miles) in width, and 996 kilometers (619 miles) in height.
  • Surface: Haumea's surface is believed to be covered in crystalline water ice, which gives it a high reflectivity, making it one of the brightest objects in the Kuiper Belt.

Named After

Haumea is named after the Hawaiian goddess of childbirth and fertility.

Rotation and Satellites

Haumea is known for its extremely rapid rotation. It completes a full rotation on its axis in just about 3.9 hours, which is one of the fastest rotation rates in the solar system. This rapid rotation contributes to its unusual shape.

Haumea has two known small moons: Hi'iaka and Namaka, which were discovered in 2005 and 2006, respectively. These moons are named after Hawaiian goddesses and are thought to be remnants of a past collision that resulted in Haumea's elongated shape.
