Ceres: Dwarf Planet

Discovery

Ceres was discovered on January 1, 1801, by Italian astronomer Giuseppe Piazzi. Initially, it was considered a planet, but later it was reclassified as an asteroid. In 2006, Ceres was reclassified again as a dwarf planet by the International Astronomical Union (IAU).

Location

Ceres is located in the asteroid belt, a region of the solar system between the orbits of Mars and Jupiter. It is the largest object in the asteroid belt.

Characteristics

  • Diameter: Ceres has a diameter of about 940 kilometers (580 miles), making it the largest object in the asteroid belt and the smallest dwarf planet.
  • Surface: Ceres has a diverse surface with large impact craters, mountains, and bright spots that are believed to be deposits of salt minerals. The bright spots have intrigued scientists and suggest the presence of subsurface water ice.
  • Water Ice: Ceres is known to contain significant amounts of water ice beneath its surface, which has led to scientific interest in its potential for hosting water and possibly life.

Named After

Ceres is named after the Roman goddess of agriculture.
