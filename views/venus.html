Venus

Venus is often called Earth's "sister planet" due to its similar size and composition, but it has a vastly different and hostile environment.

Key Characteristics

  • Orbital Period: Approximately 225 Earth days
  • Surface Temperature: Extremely hot, with temperatures exceeding 900 degrees Fahrenheit (475 degrees Celsius)
  • Atmosphere: Thick and primarily composed of carbon dioxide, with clouds of sulfuric acid

Surface Features

Venus has a rocky surface with numerous volcanoes, impact craters, and vast plains. It also exhibits a runaway greenhouse effect due to its thick atmosphere, leading to its extreme temperatures.

Exploration

Several spacecraft have visited Venus, including NASA's Magellan mission, which mapped the planet's surface with radar. Future missions aim to study Venus's atmosphere and surface in more detail.

Interesting Facts

  • Venus rotates very slowly and in the opposite direction of most planets, causing extremely long days and nights.
  • It is one of the brightest objects in the night sky and is often referred to as the "Evening Star" or "Morning Star," depending on its position relative to Earth.
