Mercury

Mercury is the smallest planet in our solar system and is located closest to the Sun.

Characteristics

  • Size: Diameter of about 4,880 kilometers (3,032 miles)
  • Surface: Heavily cratered due to impacts from space debris
  • Atmosphere: Very thin and almost negligible

Extreme Temperatures

Mercury experiences extreme temperature variations. Daytime temperatures can reach up to 430°C (800°F), while nighttime temperatures can drop to around -180°C (-290°F).

Rotation and Orbit

Mercury has a slow rotation and completes three rotations on its axis for every two orbits around the Sun. This results in its day being longer than its year.

Polar Water Ice

Despite its proximity to the Sun, some deep craters near the poles of Mercury contain water ice. These craters are in permanent shadow and have maintained the ice.

Magnetic Field and Moons

Mercury has a weak magnetic field and no moons.
