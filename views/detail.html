Our Solar System

Discover the Planets

  • The Sun The Sun is the center of our solar system and a blazing ball of nuclear fusion. It provides the energy that sustains life on Earth and all other planets.
  • Pluto Pluto was once considered the ninth planet but is now classified as a dwarf planet at the edge of the solar system. It's known for its remote and icy environment.
  • Saturn Saturn is famous for its stunning ring system made up of ice and rock particles. It's the sixth planet from the Sun and has numerous moons, including Titan.
  • Mercury Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and is known for its quick orbit and extreme temperature variations. It's a rocky planet without much of an atmosphere.
  • Mars Mars is known for its distinctive red appearance. It has polar ice caps and evidence of past liquid water flows, making it a target for future exploration.
  • Uranus Uranus is a unique planet in our solar system due to its extreme tilt, which causes it to rotate almost on its side. It's composed mostly of hydrogen and helium.
  • Venus Venus is a scorching planet with a thick atmosphere that traps heat, making it the hottest planet in our solar system. It's often called Earth's "sister planet."
  • Jupiter Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system and has a powerful magnetic field. It's known for its swirling bands of clouds and the Great Red Spot, a massive storm.
  • Earth Earth is the only known planet to support life. It has a diverse range of ecosystems, a protective atmosphere, and is home to a wide variety of species, including humans.

The Sun

Center of Our Solar System

The Sun is a massive ball of hot plasma that emits light and heat, providing energy to all the planets in our solar system.

Mercury

The Swiftest Planet

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and is known for its quick orbit and extreme temperature variations.

Venus

The Hot and Cloudy Planet

Venus is a scorching planet with a thick atmosphere that traps heat, making it the hottest planet in our solar system.

Earth

The Blue Planet

Earth is the only known planet to support life. It has a diverse range of ecosystems and a protective atmosphere.

Mars

The Red Planet

Mars is known for its distinctive red appearance. It has polar ice caps and evidence of past liquid water flows.

Jupiter

The Giant of Our Solar System

Jupiter is the largest planet and has a powerful magnetic field. It's known for its swirling bands of clouds and the Great Red Spot.

Saturn

The Ringed Planet

Saturn is famous for its stunning ring system made up of ice and rock particles. It has numerous moons, including Titan.

Uranus

The Tilted Ice Giant

Uranus is unique for its extreme tilt, which causes it to rotate almost on its side. It's composed mostly of hydrogen and helium.

Neptune

The Windy Blue Planet

Neptune is the farthest planet from the Sun and has a striking blue color. It has powerful winds and a large dark storm called the Great Dark Spot.

Pluto

The Windy Blue Planet

Pluto was discovered in 1930 by astronomer Clyde Tombaugh. In 2015, NASA's New Horizons spacecraft provided detailed images and data about Pluto during its flyby.
