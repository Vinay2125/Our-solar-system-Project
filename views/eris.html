Eris: Dwarf Planet

Discovery

Eris was discovered on January 5, 2005, by a team of astronomers led by Mike Brown at the Palomar Observatory in California. Its discovery led to the reclassification of Pluto as a dwarf planet.

Location

Eris is located in the outer regions of the solar system in a region known as the scattered disc, which is beyond the Kuiper Belt. It is one of the most distant known objects in the solar system.

Characteristics

  • Diameter: Eris has a diameter of about 2,326 kilometers (1,445 miles), making it one of the largest dwarf planets in the solar system.
  • Surface: Eris's surface composition is not well known, but it is believed to be covered in a layer of frozen methane and other ices.
  • Named After: Eris is named after the Greek goddess of strife and discord.
