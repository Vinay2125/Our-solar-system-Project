Makemake: Dwarf Planet

Discovery

Makemake was discovered on March 31, 2005, by a team of astronomers led by Mike Brown at the Palomar Observatory. It is one of the largest known objects in the Kuiper Belt.

Location

Makemake is located in the Kuiper Belt, a region of the solar system beyond the orbit of Neptune that is home to many small icy objects.

Characteristics

  • Diameter: Makemake has a diameter of about 1,430 kilometers (888 miles).
  • Surface: Makemake's surface is believed to be covered in frozen methane and ethane, which gives it a reddish color.
  • Named After: Makemake is named after the creation deity of the Rapa Nui people of Easter Island.

Atmosphere

Makemake is thought to have a thin atmosphere of methane, but it has not been extensively studied due to its distance from Earth.
